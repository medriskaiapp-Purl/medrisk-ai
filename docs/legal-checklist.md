# Legal Checklist — Before Going Live

## Employment Contract Review

MedRisk AI is in the medical device regulatory space. If your employer is also in medical devices, check these clauses:

### What to look for in your contract

1. **Non-compete clause (clause de non-concurrence)**
   - Does it prohibit side activities in the same industry?
   - Does it apply only AFTER employment ends, or also DURING?
   - French law: non-compete must be limited in scope, duration, and geography, and must include financial compensation. If no compensation → clause is void.

2. **IP assignment clause (clause de cession de PI)**
   - Does it assign ALL inventions to employer, or only work-related ones?
   - French law (Code de la propriété intellectuelle, L611-7): employer owns inventions made "in the course of duties" or using employer resources. Personal projects on personal time/equipment are typically yours.
   - Key question: Did you use ANY employer resources (laptop, software, data, time) for MedRisk AI?

3. **Exclusivity clause (clause d'exclusivité)**
   - Does your contract require full-time exclusivity?
   - French law: exclusivity clauses must be justified and proportionate.

4. **Side activity declaration (activité accessoire)**
   - Some contracts require you to DECLARE side activities even if not prohibited.
   - Check if there's a notification requirement.

### Risk assessment

| Scenario | Risk Level | Action |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Contract has no non-compete or IP clause | LOW | Proceed, but keep separation clean |
| Contract has non-compete but no compensation | LOW | Clause likely void under French law, but get legal advice |
| Contract requires declaring side activities | MEDIUM | Declare to employer before going public |
| Contract assigns all IP regardless of scope | HIGH | Get written permission or legal advice before launch |
| You used employer resources (laptop, time) | HIGH | Do NOT launch until resolved |

### Separation checklist

- [ ] MedRisk AI was built on personal computer, personal time
- [ ] No Chipiron data, code, or resources were used
- [ ] No overlap with specific Chipiron IP (coil designs, noise algorithms, etc.)
- [ ] MedRisk AI is a GENERIC regulatory tool, not specific to ULF-MRI
- [ ] New email, new GitHub, new payment account — zero connection

### If in doubt

- Consult a French employment lawyer (avocat en droit du travail)
- Cost: 100-300 EUR for a single consultation
- Ask specifically: "Can I run a side business in medical device regulatory software while employed at a medical device company?"
